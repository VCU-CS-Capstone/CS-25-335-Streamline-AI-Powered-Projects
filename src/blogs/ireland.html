Discovering Ireland

Experience the magic of the Emerald Isle – from vibrant cities to windswept coasts

Must‑Visit Destinations

Ireland blends lively cities, story‑book villages, and rugged scenery. Pin these spots to your map:

  • Dublin – Georgian squares, Trinity College’s Book of Kells, Guinness Storehouse, and the pubs of Temple Bar.
  • Galway – Bohemian streets alive with buskers, fresh oysters on the Spanish Arch, and day‑trips to Connemara.
  • Cork – Sample treats at the English Market, then kiss the Blarney Stone for eloquence.
  • Killarney – Lakeside hikes in Killarney National Park and a launch‑point for the Ring of Kerry drive.
  • Belfast – Titanic Belfast museum, Black‑Cab Troubles tours, and the basalt columns of Giant’s Causeway nearby.

Irish Flavors & Pub Culture

Hearty fare and convivial pubs keep spirits high on drizzly days:

  • Pub Sessions – Order a creamy Guinness and tap your foot to live trad music after 9 PM.
  • Irish Stew – Lamb, root veg, and thyme simmered for hours—find a bowl at The Brazen Head, Dublin’s oldest pub.
  • Seafood – Slurp Galway Bay oysters or chowder on the Wild Atlantic Way.
  • Boxty – Potato pancakes from the northwest, stuffed with beef & Guinness pie filling.
  • Whiskey Trails – Tour Jameson Midleton, Bushmills, or new craft distilleries like Teeling.

Travel Tips

  • When to go: May–June & Sept offer long daylight and moderate crowds; pack layers year‑round.
  • Getting around: Trains link major cities; rent a car for coastal byways (drive on the left!).
  • Money: Euro (€) in the Republic; Pound Sterling (£) in Northern Ireland—cards widely accepted.
  • Heritage Card: OPW pass grants entry to 45+ historic sites—pays for itself after 3–4 castles.
  • Language: English everywhere; look for bilingual Gaelic road signs in the west.

Festivals & Events

  • St Patrick’s Day (17 Mar) – Emerald parades and rivers dyed green nationwide.
  • Galway International Arts Festival (July) – Theater, music, and giant street puppets.
  • Cork Jazz Festival (Oct) – Sax riffs spill from every pub corner.
  • Bloomsday (16 Jun) – Joyce fans in straw boaters retrace Leopold Bloom’s route through Dublin.
  • Puck Fair (Aug) – A goat is crowned king for three days in Killorglin, County Kerry.

Iconic Landscapes

  • Cliffs of Moher – 214 m sea‑cliffs thundering with Atlantic waves.
  • Ring of Kerry – 179 km circuit of beaches, lakes, and stone forts.
  • Giant’s Causeway – 40,000 basalt columns formed by ancient lava flows.
  • Connemara – Wild blanket bogs, Twelve Bens peaks, and the sky road to Clifden.
  • Dingle Peninsula – Slea Head Drive vistas and maybe a dolphin greeting in Dingle Bay.
  • Wicklow Mountains – Glendalough monastic ruins set in glacial valleys south of Dublin.

From windswept headlands to fireside fiddle tunes, Ireland charms at every turn. Slán go fóill—see you soon on the Emerald Isle!
