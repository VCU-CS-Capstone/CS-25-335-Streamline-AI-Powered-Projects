Exploring Costa Rica

Pura vida vibes: volcano treks, cloud‑forest zip‑lines, and surf‑washed beaches

Must‑Visit Destinations

Costa Rica packs 5% of the planet’s biodiversity into a country smaller than West Virginia. Don’t miss these highlights:

  • Arenal Volcano & La Fortuna: Hike lava fields, soak in hot springs, and rappel waterfalls in this adventure hub.
  • Monteverde Cloud Forest: Walk hanging bridges above mist‑shrouded treetops listening for resplendent quetzals.
  • Manuel Antonio National Park: White‑sand coves, capuchin monkeys, and sloths within the country’s most popular park.
  • Tortuguero: Glide canals spotting caimans and witness green sea turtles nesting (July–Oct) on Caribbean shores.
  • Guanacaste Beaches: Surf breaks at Tamarindo, sunset sails from Playas del Coco, and luxe resorts on the Papagayo Peninsula.

Costa Rican Cuisine

Simple, fresh, and comforting—Tico food fuels eco‑adventures:

  • Gallo Pinto: Rice & beans sautéed with cilantro and onions, served with eggs and plantains for breakfast.
  • Casado: Lunch plate of protein (fish, chicken, or beef) plus rice, beans, salad, and fried sweet plantain.
  • Ceviche Tico: Corvina cured in lime with cilantro and peppers—pair with an icy Imperial beer.
  • Chifrijo: Bar‑snack bowl of rice, beans, fried pork, pico de gallo, and tortilla chips.
  • Café Britt & Tarrazú Coffee: Tour plantations in the Central Valley and sample world‑class arabica.

Travel Tips

Plan like a pro with these pura vida pointers:

  • Season: Dry Dec–April; green season May–Nov (best for fewer crowds and lush landscapes); Caribbean coast differs.
  • Currency: Costa Rican Colón (CRC); USD widely accepted—carry small bills for sodas (local diners) and buses.
  • Transportation: Shuttle vans and 4×4 rentals navigate winding roads; domestic Sansa flights save time.
  • Wildlife Etiquette: Keep 3‑meter distance, never feed animals, and use red filters for turtle nesting.
  • Safety: Tap water safe in most areas; petty theft can occur—use hotel safes and avoid leaving items in cars.

Where to Stay

From canopy‑level eco‑lodges to beachfront bungalows:

  • Eco‑Lodges: Nayara Gardens (Arenal) and Lapa Rios (Osa) set the standard for sustainability and wildlife immersion.
  • Surf Hostels: Selina in Tamarindo or Santa Teresa offers coworking and wave‑chasing camaraderie.
  • Luxury Resorts: Four Seasons Papagayo and Andaz Peninsula Papagayo overlook turquoise bays.
  • Boutique Hotels: Hotel Belmar’s alpine‑style charm in Monteverde or El Mangroove’s chic suites in Guanacaste.
  • Glamping: Sleep in transparent domes at Arenal’s Chachagua Rainforest or jungle treehouses in Puerto Viejo.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Celebrate pura vida spirit year‑round:

  • Fiestas Palmares (Jan): Two‑week carnival with concerts, bull rides (non‑lethal), and horse parades.
  • Envision Festival (late Feb): Music, yoga, and art gathering on Uvita’s rainforest‑meets‑beach setting.
  • Día de la Independencia (Sept 15): Torch relays, lantern parades, and folk dances across towns.
  • Tope Nacional de Caballos (Dec 26): San José streets fill with beautifully groomed horses and riders.
  • Turtle Nesting Walks: Join guided patrols at Ostional (olive ridley arribadas) or Tortuguero (green turtles).
