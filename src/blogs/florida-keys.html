Exploring the Florida Keys

Island‑hopping on the Overseas Highway: coral reefs, key lime pies, and sunsets that stop traffic

Must‑Visit Destinations

The 113‑mile island chain strings together unique communities—here’s the low‑down north to south:

  • Key Largo: Dive John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park, the first underwater park in the U.S., and photograph the Christ of the Abyss statue.
  • Islamorada: Sport‑fishing capital—charter boats chase sailfish while Robbie’s Marina lets you hand‑feed giant tarpon.
  • Marathon: Walk the restored Old Seven Mile Bridge to Pigeon Key, visit the Turtle Hospital, and kayak mangrove tunnels.
  • Big Pine & Lower Keys: Spot endangered Key deer in National Key Deer Refuge and snorkel Looe Key’s vibrant reef.
  • Key West: Tour Hemingway House cats, toast sunset at Mallory Square, and bike pastel streets to Southernmost Point.

Keys Cuisine & Drinks

Fresh seafood and citrusy sweets fuel tropical days:

  • Conch Fritters & Chowder: Try them at Mrs. Mac’s Kitchen or Conch Republic Seafood Co.
  • Key Lime Pie: Debate meringue vs. whipped cream at Kermit’s or Blue Heaven—always graham‑cracker crust.
  • Stone Crab Claws: In season Oct–May; crack and dip in mustard sauce at Keys Fisheries.
  • Rum Runners: Islamorada’s Holiday Isle invented this fruity cocktail—order one at the Tiki Bar overlooking the marina.
  • Fresh‑Catch Sandwiches: Blackened mahi or hogfish on Cuban bread with island slaw at Hogfish Bar & Grill.

Travel Tips

Navigate the Overseas Highway like a local:

  • Best Time: December–April dry season has pleasant temps; June–Nov is hurricane season with humid heat and afternoon storms.
  • Traffic: Single‑lane US‑1 can clog—start early and build buffer time for bridge selfies.
  • Snorkel Etiquette: Wear reef‑safe sunscreen, don’t touch coral, and maintain buoyancy over seagrass beds.
  • Sun Protection: UV index soars year‑round—wide‑brim hats and hydration are musts.
  • Park Passes: Reserve in advance for Dry Tortugas National Park seaplane or ferry from Key West.

Where to Stay

Find your island base, from luxe resorts to rustic camps:

  • Key Largo Resorts: Playa Largo & Bungalows Key Largo offer over‑water hammocks and sunset fire pits.
  • Islamorada Chic: The Morada Bay Beach Café cottages and Cheeca Lodge’s palm‑lined pools.
  • Marathon Mid‑Keys: Tranquility Bay’s beach houses or Bahia Honda State Park campsites under star‑filled skies.
  • Key West Historic Inns: Kimpton Winslow’s Bungalows and Marquesa Hotel mix Conch architecture with modern luxury.
  • Live‑Aboard Boats: Sleep on catamarans moored in mangrove‑sheltered marinas for a unique stay.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Island spirit shines through quirky festivals and nature encounters:

  • Fantasy Fest (Oct): Key West’s flamboyant costumed street party rivals Mardi Gras.
  • Hemingway Days (July): Look‑alike contests, running of the bulls spoof, and marlin tournaments honor Papa.
  • Key West Songwriters Festival (May): 200+ artists perform intimate sets in bars and boats.
  • Lobster Mini Season (last Wed–Thu July): Recreational dive for spiny lobsters before commercial traps drop.
  • Conch Republic Independence Celebration (April): Tongue‑in‑cheek secession anniversary with bed races and sea battles.
