Exploring Panamá

Canal marvels, Caribbean isles, cloud‑forest coffee, and Afro‑Latino rhythms in the land of two oceans

Must‑Visit Destinations

Panamá bridges continents and seas—here are essential stops:

  • Panama Canal & Casco Viejo: Watch mega‑ships transit Miraflores Locks, then wander colonial plazas and rooftop bars in the UNESCO old quarter.
  • San Blas (Guna Yala) Islands: Sail turquoise waters and sleep in palm‑fringed cabañas managed by the indigenous Guna people.
  • Bocas del Toro: Caribbean surf, bioluminescent bays, and over‑water hostels—cycle Isla Colón or snorkel Starfish Beach.
  • Boquete & Volcán Barú: Sip Geisha coffee on cloud‑forest farms and summit Panama’s highest peak for sunrise views of both oceans.
  • Parque Nacional Coiba: Dive UNESCO‑listed waters teeming with whale sharks, manta rays, and coral reefs dubbed the “Galápagos of Central America.”

Panamanian Flavors

A fusion of Afro‑Caribbean, Indigenous, and Spanish influences:

  • Ceviche de Corvina: Grab a cup at Panama City’s Mercado de Mariscos drizzled with tangy chombo pepper sauce.
  • Sancocho: Hearty chicken‑yucca soup spiced with culantro—Panamá’s national comfort food.
  • Raspao: Shaved‑ice street treat topped with fruit syrup and condensed milk—perfect for Canal‑side heat.
  • Carimañolas & Hojaldres: Yuca fritters stuffed with beef and fluffy fry‑bread for breakfast in the Interior.
  • Seco Herrerano & Ron Abuelo: Sample sugar‑cane spirits in cocktails like chicheme colao or straight up with lime.

Travel Tips

Make the most of your isthmus adventure:

  • Season: Dry mid‑Dec–April; green season May–Nov (Caribbean side sees showers year‑round).
  • Currency: Balboa (PAB) pegged 1:1 to USD; US dollars circulate—coins unique, bills same.
  • Transport: Domestic Air Panamá flights shorten jungle journeys; Metro & Metrobus ease city traffic; 4×4 required for San Blas road.
  • Entry: Proof of onward travel and 3‑month passport validity required for most visitors.
  • Eco‑Respect: Reef‑safe sunscreen, no single‑use plastics in Guna Yala, and leave no trace on cloud‑forest trails.

Where to Stay

From skyscraper suites to over‑water huts:

  • Panama City Skyline: JW Marriott’s sail‑shaped tower and W Panama’s art‑filled rooms overlook the Pacific entrance.
  • Casco Viejo Boutiques: American Trade Hotel and La Concordia blend colonial charm with jazz bars and rooftop pools.
  • Over‑Water Bungalows: Isla Palenque in the Gulf of Chiriquí and Azul Paradise in Bocas deliver barefoot luxury.
  • Coffee‑Farm Stays: Finca Lerida and The Haven Spa in Boquete offer cool‑climate retreats among orchids and quetzals.
  • Dive Lodges: Coiba Dive Center’s Santa Catalina cabins or Kalu Yala’s jungle eco‑village for off‑grid vibes.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Feel the rhythm of Panamá:

  • Carnaval (4 days before Ash Wednesday): Las Tablas erupts with pollera gowns, culeco water trucks, and tamborito beats.
  • Festival de la Mejorana (Sept): Guararé celebrates folkloric dances, devil masks, and mejorana guitar contests.
  • Sea Turtle Season (July–Nov): Volunteer night patrols at Playa La Marte or Isla Cañas on the Azuero Peninsula.
  • Panama Jazz Festival (Jan): Danilo Pérez hosts global musicians for week‑long concerts and clinics in the capital.
  • Emberá Village Visits: Journey up the Chagres River for traditional dances, tagua carvings, and rainforest medicine walks.
