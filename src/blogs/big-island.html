Exploring Big Island

Witness fiery volcanoes, star‑filled skies, and lush valleys on the youngest Hawaiian isle

Must‑Visit Destinations on the Big Island

The Big Island is larger than all the other Hawaiian islands combined, offering diverse climates and adventures. Don’t miss these highlights:

  • Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park: Hike lava fields, peer into Kīlauea caldera, and (conditions permitting) view glowing lava after dark.
  • Mauna Kea Summit & Stargazing: At 13,803 ft, it’s one of the world’s premier astronomical sites—sunset from the summit and stargazing at the Visitor Information Station are unforgettable.
  • Kona Coffee Belt: Tour family farms along the slopes of Hualālai and sample world‑famous 100% Kona coffee.
  • Waipiʻo Valley: Gaze over emerald cliffs and taro fields from the lookout or join a guided tour down to the black‑sand beach below.
  • South Point & Papakōlea Green Sand Beach: Stand at the southernmost point of the USA, then trek to one of only four green‑sand beaches on Earth.

Island Flavors & Dining Experiences

From fresh‑caught poke to farm‑to‑table fare, the Big Island’s cuisine is a celebration of local bounty:

  • Poke: Grab ahi or tako (octopus) poke at Da Poke Shack in Kailua‑Kona—often ranked among the best in Hawaiʻi.
  • Loco Moco: A hearty plate of rice, hamburger patty, fried egg, and gravy—try the classic at Cafe 100 in Hilo, birthplace of the dish.
  • Huli‑Huli Chicken: Succulent chicken grilled over kiawe wood at roadside stands like the one near Punaluʻu Black Sand Beach on weekends.
  • Farm‑to‑Fork: Merriman’s in Waimea pioneered Hawaiʻi Regional Cuisine, spotlighting island‑grown produce and ranch meats.
  • Kona Brewing Co.: Sip Liquid Aloha at the original brewery pub paired with island‑inspired pizzas.

Travel Tips for the Big Island

Navigate the island’s vastness and microclimates with these savvy suggestions:

  • Best Time to Visit: The west (Kona) coast is sunny year‑round; whale season runs Dec–Apr; visit shoulder months Sept–Oct for fewer crowds and calm seas.
  • Getting Around: A rental car is essential—Circle Island drives can exceed 300 miles. Four‑wheel drive recommended for Mauna Kea summit.
  • Microclimates: Pack layers—temperatures can swing from tropical beaches to near‑freezing atop Mauna Kea in one day.
  • Respecting Pele: Stay on marked trails and never take lava rocks home; it’s illegal and culturally disrespectful.
  • Reef‑Safe Sunscreen: Hawaiʻi bans sunscreens with oxybenzone & octinoxate—choose mineral‑based formulas to protect coral reefs.

Accommodation Options

Whether you crave resort luxury or off‑grid serenity, the Big Island has you covered:

  • Kohala Coast Resorts: Bask in year‑round sunshine at the Fairmont Orchid, Mauna Lani, or the legendary Mauna Kea Beach Hotel.
  • Boutique B&Bs: Volcano Village offers cozy rainforest cottages minutes from the park entrance.
  • Eco‑Lodges: Experience sustainable living at Hoʻokena Beach or Puakea Ranch with solar power and farm animals.
  • Vacation Rentals: Oceanfront condos in Kailua‑Kona or cliffside homes near Hilo provide space for families and groups.
  • Camping & Cabins: Reserve Nāmakanipaio cabins inside the national park for a night under starry skies.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Connect with Native Hawaiian traditions and island celebrations:

  • Merrie Monarch Festival (Easter week): The world’s premier hula competition transforms Hilo into a cultural epicenter.
  • Kona Coffee Cultural Festival (Nov): Parades, farm tours, and cupping contests honor 200 years of coffee heritage.
  • Ironman World Championship (Oct): Cheer elite triathletes as they swim, bike, and run through Kailua‑Kona’s heat and wind.
  • First Friday Hilo & Kailua Village Strolls: Monthly street fairs showcase local artisans, live music, and ʻono (delicious) eats.
  • Lava Boat & Night Manta Ray Tours: When lava meets ocean (eruption dependent) or glide with gentle giants off Kona coast after sunset.
