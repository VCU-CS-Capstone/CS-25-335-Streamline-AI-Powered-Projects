The Beauty of the Bahamas

Turquoise shallows, pink‑sand beaches, and island‑hopping adventures await

Top Activities in the Bahamas

The Bahamas is a playground for water sports, beach relaxation, and natural wonders:

  • Exuma Cays Land & Sea Park: Snorkel vibrant reefs inside the country’s first marine reserve.
  • Pig Beach: Splash alongside the famous swimming pigs on Big Major Cay.
  • Dean’s Blue Hole: Free‑dive—or simply marvel at—the world’s second‑deepest blue hole.
  • Atlantis Paradise Island: Tackle Aquaventure water slides, explore marine lagoons, or try your luck at the casino.

Dining in the Bahamas

Fresh conch, rock lobster, and tropical flavors headline Bahamian menus:

  • Fish Fry at Arawak Cay: Join locals for cracked conch, sky‑juice cocktails, and Junkanoo beats.
  • Graycliff Restaurant: Dine in an 18th‑century mansion boasting one of the Caribbean’s largest wine cellars.
  • The Poop Deck: Casual harbor‑view spot serving grilled snapper and Kalik beer.
  • Sunset Grill, Nassau: Savor conch fritters while watching the sun dip behind Paradise Island.

Local Tips for Travelers

  • Best Season: December–April offers dry, breezy weather; June–Nov is wetter with hurricane risk but fewer crowds.
  • Currency: Bahamian Dollar (BSD) pegged 1:1 to USD—both accepted interchangeably.
  • Island Hopping: Ferries and small planes link 700 islands; plan extra time for weather delays.
  • Reef Etiquette: Wear reef‑safe sunscreen and never stand on coral.

Where to Stay

  • Atlantis Paradise Island: Iconic mega‑resort with 141‑acre water park and marine habitat.
  • SLS Baha Mar: Stylish retreat within Nassau’s Baha Mar complex.
  • The Ocean Club, A Four Seasons Resort: James Bond‑famous luxury amid Versailles‑inspired gardens.
  • Pink Sands Resort: Boutique cottages fronting Harbour Island’s rosy shoreline.
