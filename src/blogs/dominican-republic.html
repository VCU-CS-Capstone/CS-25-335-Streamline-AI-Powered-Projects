Discovering the Dominican Republic

Discover the perfect blend of adventure, relaxation, and rich culture

Top Activities in the Dominican Republic

The Dominican Republic offers an incredible variety of activities for travelers of all interests, from beach relaxation to thrilling adventures and cultural immersion.

  • Visit Saona Island – Take a catamaran excursion to this stunning protected nature reserve with pristine beaches and crystal‑clear waters.
  • Explore Santo Domingo's Colonial Zone – Wander through the oldest European settlement in the Americas, a UNESCO World Heritage site with historic buildings dating back to the 16th century.
  • Damajagua Waterfalls – Embark on an exhilarating adventure climbing and jumping down a series of 27 waterfalls near Puerto Plata.
  • Visit Altos de Chavón – Explore this remarkable recreation of a 16th‑century Mediterranean village overlooking the Chavón River.
  • Kiteboarding in Cabarete – Try your hand at kiteboarding or windsurfing in one of the world's premier destinations for these water sports.
  • Whale Watching in Samaná – Witness the majestic humpback whales during their annual migration between January and March.

Where to Dine

Dominican cuisine is a delightful fusion of Spanish, African, and Taíno influences, with an emphasis on fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and flavorful stews.

  • La Yola Restaurant – Dine in a boat‑shaped restaurant over the water in Punta Cana, enjoying fresh seafood with spectacular views.
  • Pat'e Palo – Experience European‑inspired cuisine in the oldest tavern in the Americas, located in Santo Domingo's Colonial Zone.
  • Mesón de la Cava – Enjoy an unforgettable meal in a natural limestone cave in Santo Domingo, featuring international cuisine with Dominican touches.
  • Restaurante El Conuco – Savor authentic Dominican food while enjoying traditional music and dance performances.
  • Jalao – Taste modern interpretations of classic Dominican dishes in a lively atmosphere in Santo Domingo.
  • Beachside Seafood Shacks – Don't miss the humble beachside eateries serving fresh‑caught fish, frequently found in Bayahibe, Las Terrenas, and other coastal areas.

Local Tips for Travelers

Make the most of your Dominican Republic experience with these insider tips from locals and seasoned travelers.

  • Best Time to Visit – The ideal time to visit is during the dry season from November to March. Avoid September and October, which are the peak of hurricane season.
  • Moving Around – For short distances within towns, consider using motoconchos (motorcycle taxis), but negotiate the price before getting on. For longer journeys, guaguas (public minibuses) are inexpensive but often crowded.
  • Resort Alternatives – While all‑inclusive resorts are popular, consider staying in boutique hotels in Las Terrenas, Cabarete, or Las Galeras for a more authentic experience.
  • Language Barrier – Spanish is the official language; learning a few basic phrases will enrich your experience and is appreciated by locals.
  • Currency Usage – The Dominican peso is official, but tourist areas accept USD. You'll generally get better value using pesos away from resorts.
  • Beach Safety – Always check for flags indicating sea conditions before swimming; some beaches have strong currents.

The Dominican Republic offers far more than just beautiful beaches and all‑inclusive resorts. Venture beyond tourist zones to discover a country rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, with some of the warmest and most welcoming people in the Caribbean.
