Exploring the Amalfi Coast

Pastel cliff‑side villages, lemon‑scented lanes, and Tyrrhenian blues on Italy’s most scenic shoreline

Must‑Visit Destinations

The 50‑km coastal road strings together jewels—each with its own vibe:

  • Positano: Climb color‑washed stairways, shop linen boutiques, and sip spritzes on Spiaggia Grande.
  • Amalfi: Tour the 9th‑century Duomo di Sant’Andrea and sample fresh sfogliatella by the piazza.
  • Ravello: Stroll Villa Cimbrone’s Terrace of Infinity and catch classical concerts at Villa Rufolo.
  • Path of the Gods Hike: Trek from Bomerano to Nocelle for epic cliff‑edge panoramas.
  • Capri Day Trip: Ferry from Positano, cruise the Blue Grotto, and ride the chairlift up Monte Solaro.

Coastal Flavors

Citrus groves and sea harvests dictate the menu:

  • Limoncello: Sip the zesty digestivo at family‑run Limoncello di Capri distillery.
  • Scialatielli ai Frutti di Mare: Handmade pasta tossed with mixed seafood at Trattoria da Gemma.
  • Delizia al Limone: Sponge cake domes soaked in lemon cream—find the best at Pasticceria Sal De Riso in Minori.
  • Fior di Latte Mozzarella: Farm‑fresh cheese from Agerola paired with sun‑dried tomatoes.
  • Neapolitan Pizza Side Trip: 1‑hour drive to Naples for a Vera Pizza Napoletana at Sorbillo on Via dei Tribunali.

Travel Tips

Navigate those hairpin bends with ease:

  • Best Time: Late April–June & Sept–Oct for warm seas and fewer tour buses; July–Aug peak crowds.
  • Transport: SITA buses and ferries link towns—driving is scenic but parking scarce; consider private transfer from Naples airport.
  • Boat Taxis: Avoid road traffic with seasonal hop‑on ferries between Positano, Amalfi, and Capri.
  • Footwear: Cobblestone lanes demand sturdy sandals—heels stay home.
  • Cash: Small trattorias prefer euros; ATMs can run dry on weekends—plan ahead.

Where to Stay

Wake up to sea‑stack vistas:

  • Luxury Icons: Le Sirenuse in Positano and Caruso Belmond in Ravello offer infinity pools above the clouds.
  • Boutique Charm: Hotel Marincanto’s cliff‑side terraces or Amalfi’s NH Convento di Amalfi housed in a 13th‑century monastery.
  • Family‑Run B&Bs: Casa Buonocore and Villa Maria deliver warm hospitality and homemade limoncello cake.
  • Agriturismi: Stay among lemon groves at Agriturismo Sant’Alfonso near Furore.
  • Budget Hostels: Agerola’s BeB Divino boasts Path of the Gods trailhead access and pizza evenings.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Soak up la dolce vita traditions:

  • Ravello Festival (July–Sept): Open‑air concerts on Villa Rufolo’s stage suspended above the sea.
  • Regatta of the Ancient Maritime Republics (June, rotating cities): Amalfi competes in medieval boat races and parades.
  • Feast of Sant’Andrea (27 June & 30 Nov): Amalfi’s patron saint celebrated with fireworks and processions.
  • Lemon Harvest (May): Join tours of terraced groves and taste fresh granita al limone.
  • Paper Museum Workshops: Make handmade bambagina paper in Amalfi’s Valle dei Mulini.
