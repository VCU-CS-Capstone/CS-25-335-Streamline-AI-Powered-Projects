Exploring Tuscany, Italy

Cypress‑lined hills, Renaissance art, and Chianti sips across Italy’s rolling heartland

Must‑Visit Destinations

From medieval hill towns to art‑packed cities, Tuscany offers postcard moments at every turn:

  • Florence: Gaze at Michelangelo’s David in the Accademia, climb Brunelleschi’s Duomo, and shop leather in San Lorenzo.
  • Siena: Wander fan‑shaped Piazza del Campo and trace Palio horse‑race rivalries in contrada museums.
  • Chianti Wine Road: Drive SR222 between Greve and Radda, tasting sangiovese at family vineyards.
  • Val d’Orcia: Snap the cypress road to Agriturismo Poggio Covili, soak in Bagno Vignoni hot springs, and explore Pienza’s pecorino shops.
  • Pisa & Lucca: Climb the Leaning Tower early, then cycle Lucca’s Renaissance walls at golden hour.

Tuscan Flavors

Simple, seasonal, and steeped in tradition:

  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: 1‑kg T‑bone grilled rare over olive‑wood embers at Trattoria da Burde.
  • Ribollita: Hearty bean‑kale bread soup perfect for autumn evenings.
  • Pappardelle al Cinghiale: Wide noodles coated in slow‑braised wild boar ragù in Montalcino.
  • Gelato Origins: Taste inventive flavors at Gelateria Dondoli in San Gimignano, Gelato World Champion.
  • Chianti Classico & Brunello: Book cellar tours at Castello di Brolio and Banfi for sangiovese masterclasses.

Travel Tips

Roam the region like a local:

  • Best Time: May–June & Sept–Oct for vineyard colors and mild temps; July–Aug brings sunflower fields and heat.
  • Car Rental: Essential for countryside—watch ZTL restricted zones in historic centers.
  • Trains: Florence–Pisa–Lucca & Florence–Siena routes are frequent; buy regionale tickets and validate before boarding.
  • Agriturismo Stays: Farmhouse lodgings include breakfast of local honey, olive oil tastings, and cooking classes.
  • Market Days: Stock up on pecorino, truffles, and cantucci at Greve’s Saturday mercato.

Where to Stay

Wake up to rolling‑hill vistas or Renaissance rooftops:

  • Luxury Villas: Rosewood Castiglion del Bosco offers vineyard suites and a Brunello winery.
  • Historic City Hotels: Florence’s Hotel Lungarno frames Ponte Vecchio views; Siena’s Palazzo Ravizza exudes 18th‑century charm.
  • Agriturismi: Fattoria Poggio Alloro near San Gimignano lets you dine overlooking towers and vines.
  • Wine Resorts: Castello di Velona’s thermal pools sit amid Brunello vines.
  • Budget Hostels: Ostello Bello Firenze hosts rooftop aperitivo with Duomo skyline.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Live la dolce vita, Tuscan‑style:

  • Palio di Siena (2 July & 16 Aug): Bareback horse race thunders around Piazza del Campo.
  • Calcio Storico (June): Medieval football‑meets‑rugby battles in Florence’s Piazza Santa Croce.
  • Wine Harvest (Sept): Join grape‑picking and stomping festivals in Chianti and Montalcino.
  • Lucca Summer Festival (July): International rock & pop acts perform within Renaissance walls.
  • Truffle Fairs (Oct–Nov): Hunt white truffles in San Miniato and sample aromatic dishes.
