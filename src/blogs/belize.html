Exploring Belize

Barrier‑reef diving, jungle temples, and laid‑back island vibes in English‑speaking Central America

Must‑Visit Destinations in Belize

Small but mighty, Belize packs reef, rainforest, and rich culture into one passport stamp:

  • Great Blue Hole: Scuba or fly over this 1,043‑ft‑wide sinkhole made famous by Jacques Cousteau.
  • Ambergris Caye & San Pedro: Golf‑cart around beach bars, snorkel Hol Chan Marine Reserve, and meet friendly nurse sharks at Shark Ray Alley.
  • Caye Caulker: Embrace “Go Slow” mantra—sip rum at The Split and kayak mangrove shallows spotting seahorses.
  • ATM Cave: Wade through crystal water to view Mayan skeletons and pottery frozen in time deep underground.
  • Caracol & Mountain Pine Ridge: Climb Caana pyramid, then cool off at Rio On Pools amid granite cascades.

Belizean Cuisine & Dining

Creole, Garifuna, Mestizo, and Maya flavors collide on the plate:

  • Rice & Beans with Stew Chicken: Sunday staple slow‑cooked in coconut milk—find it at roadside comedores.
  • Fry Jacks: Puffy breakfast dough pockets served with jam or refried beans.
  • Hudut: Garifuna coconut fish stew with mashed plantain dumplings—best in Hopkins Village.
  • Lobster Festivals: June–July celebrations in San Pedro, Caye Caulker, and Placencia dish out grilled tails and lobster sliders.
  • Marie Sharp’s: Belize’s iconic habanero hot sauce—tour the factory near Dangriga.

Travel Tips for Belize

Handy intel for smooth sailing:

  • Language: English is official; Kriol, Spanish, and Garifuna are widely spoken.
  • Currency: Belize Dollar (BZD) is pegged 2:1 to USD; US cash accepted almost everywhere.
  • Getting Around: Water taxis link cayes; Maya Island Air & Tropic Air hop to jungle airstrips; buses are cheap but slow on mainland.
  • Season: Dry season Nov–Apr; whale sharks visit Gladden Spit Apr–Jun; hurricane risk peaks Aug–Oct.
  • Reef Protection: Reef‑safe sunscreen and no‑touch policies help preserve the UNESCO‑listed barrier reef.

Where to Stay

Lodging options span barefoot chic to jungle luxe:

  • Over‑Water Cabanas: Thatch Caye and Coco Plum Islands offer private‑island serenity.
  • Ambergris Resorts: Victoria House and Alaia Belize blend dive‑shop convenience with infinity‑pool relaxation.
  • Placencia Boutique: Naia Resort’s beach houses and Itz’ana’s villa suites face calm Caribbean waters.
  • Jungle Lodges: The Lodge at Chaa Creek and Blancaneaux (Coppola) feature riverside cottages and birding trails.
  • Budget Guesthouses: Caye Caulker’s Iguana Reef Inn and Bella’s Backpackers keep vibes social and wallets happy.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Dive into Belize’s melting‑pot heritage:

  • September Celebrations: St. George’s Caye Day (Sept 10) & Independence Day (Sept 21) ignite parades and concerts nationwide.
  • Garifuna Settlement Day (Nov 19): Drumming, dancing, and re‑enactments of 1802 arrival in Dangriga & Hopkins.
  • Belize Carnival (early Sept): Feathered costumes and soca beats flood Belize City streets.
  • Costa Maya Festival (Aug): San Pedro hosts regional pageants, concerts, and food fairs.
  • Chocolate Festival (May): Punta Gorda celebrates ancient Maya cacao with tastings and farm tours.
