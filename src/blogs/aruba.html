Exploring Aruba

Discover the sun‑kissed beaches, vibrant culture, and natural wonders of Aruba

Must-Visit Destinations in Aruba

Aruba may be a small island, but it is packed with breathtaking beaches, unique landscapes, and colorful towns. Here are some unmissable spots to add to your itinerary:

  • Oranjestad: Stroll the capital’s pastel‑hued Dutch‑Caribbean architecture, visit Fort Zoutman, and shop at Renaissance Mall.
  • Eagle Beach: Consistently ranked among the world’s best beaches, its soft white sand and iconic fofoti trees create a postcard‑perfect scene.
  • Arikok National Park: Explore desert landscapes, cacti‑studded hills, limestone caves with Arawak drawings, and the natural pool “Conchi.”
  • Palm Beach: A two‑mile stretch lined with high‑rise resorts, lively beach bars, and calm waters ideal for paddleboarding.
  • Baby Beach & San Nicolas: Relax in shallow turquoise water at Baby Beach, then admire vibrant street art in Aruba’s emerging cultural hub, San Nicolas.

Aruban Cuisine and Dining Experiences

Aruba’s food scene blends Dutch, Caribbean, Latin, and international influences. Don’t leave without tasting these local favorites:

  • Keshi Yena: A savory dish of spiced meat, olives, and raisins baked inside a hollowed‑out Edam cheese rind.
  • Pastechi: Deep‑fried pastries stuffed with cheese, chicken, or beef—Aruba’s go‑to breakfast on the run.
  • Zeerovers: Head to this fishermen’s wharf in Savaneta for ultra‑fresh snapper and shrimp sold by weight and fried to order.
  • Dutch Treats: Snack on bitterballen (crispy beef croquettes) or indulge in fluffy Dutch pancakes at local cafés.
  • Food Truck Scene: After sunset, join locals lining up at trucks like Kuchinate or Frita Dushi for loaded burgers and Caribbean BBQ.

Travel Tips for Aruba

Make the most of your One Happy Island getaway with these practical pointers:

  • Best Time to Visit: Aruba sits south of the hurricane belt, so it enjoys year‑round sunshine. Peak season is mid‑December–March; visit April–August for lower rates and fewer crowds.
  • Currency: The Aruban Florin (Afl.) is official, but US dollars are accepted almost everywhere and ATMs dispense both.
  • Languages: Papiamento and Dutch are official, while English and Spanish are widely spoken.
  • Transportation: Renting a car offers maximum freedom; otherwise, the reliable Arubus system connects major areas for under US$3 per ride.
  • Sun & Water Safety: The tap water is some of the purest in the world, and trade winds keep temps pleasant—just pack reef‑safe sunscreen to protect Aruba’s marine life.

Accommodation Options

From luxury resorts to eco‑friendly retreats, Aruba has lodging for every style and budget:

  • Beachfront Resorts: High‑rise hotels on Palm Beach like the Hilton and Hyatt Regency offer full amenities steps from the sand.
  • All‑Inclusive Escapes: Relax knowing meals and activities are covered at properties such as Barcelo Aruba or Divi Aruba.
  • Boutique Gems: Stay at chic spots like Boardwalk Boutique Hotel or Wonders Boutique for a more intimate vibe.
  • Vacation Rentals: Opt for condos or villas—great for families or longer stays—particularly around Eagle Beach and Malmok.
  • Eco‑Lodges: Experience Aruba’s rugged side at sustainable hideaways near Arikok, featuring solar power and rainwater systems.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Immerse yourself in Aruba’s lively culture through these annual festivities and local traditions:

  • Carnival (Jan–Feb): A six‑week explosion of parades, glittering costumes, and steel‑pan music culminating in the Grand Parade.
  • Bon Bini Festival: Every Tuesday evening at Fort Zoutman, enjoy folkloric dance, live music, and local crafts welcoming visitors to the island.
  • Dande Festival: Between Christmas and New Year’s, traveling musicians serenade homes with songs of goodwill for the coming year.
  • Aruba Hi‑Winds: World‑class windsurfing and kitesurfing races harness the trade winds each June/July at Fisherman’s Huts.
  • Soul Beach Music Festival: Memorial Day weekend brings international R&B artists, comedy shows, and beach parties to Palm Beach.
