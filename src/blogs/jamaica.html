The Vibrance of Jamaica

Reggae rhythms, cascading waterfalls, and Blue Mountain breezes

Top Activities in Jamaica

Jamaica bursts with adventure and culture:

  • Dunn’s River Falls: Hand‑hold your way up 600‑ft terraced cascades in Ocho Rios.
  • Blue Mountain Hike: Sunrise trek rewards you with coffee farm aromas and island‑wide vistas.
  • Bob Marley Museum: Explore Kingston’s musical shrine to the reggae legend.
  • Bamboo Rafting Martha Brae: Glide past lush jungle on a 30‑ft bamboo raft steered by a local captain.
  • Seven Mile Beach, Negril: Chill on powder sand by day, catch cliff divers at Rick’s Café by sunset.
  • Luminous Lagoon: Night‑time swim among bioluminescent dinoflagellates near Falmouth.

Dining in Jamaica

Island spices meet farm‑fresh produce:

  • Jerk Shacks: Scotch‑bonnet‑blasted chicken and pork at Boston Bay or Scotchies.
  • Ackee & Saltfish: National breakfast—try it with festival bread on the roadside.
  • Juici Patties: Flaky pastry pockets filled with beef, chicken, or curried veg.
  • Miss T’s Kitchen: Ocho Rios favorite elevating yam, callaloo, and oxtail.
  • Blue Mountain Coffee: Sip a pour‑over at Café Blue overlooking misty peaks.

Local Tips for Travelers

  • Best Time: Peak season Dec–Apr; shoulder months May & Nov see fewer crowds.
  • Currency: Jamaican Dollar (JMD); USD accepted in resorts but carry small JMD bills for street eats.
  • Transport: Route taxis are cheap—look for red license plates; agree fare first.
  • Language: English official; greet locals with “Wah gwaan?” for instant smiles.
  • Safety: Stick to licensed guides for excursions and avoid isolated beaches at night.

Where to Stay

  • Half Moon, Montego Bay: Iconic horseshoe‑shaped beach and luxe villas.
  • Rockhouse Hotel, Negril: Cliff‑edge cabins with ladders into turquoise sea.
  • Strawberry Hill, Blue Mountains: Gingerbread cottages and infinity pool in cool highlands.
  • Geejam, Port Antonio: Music‑studio boutique nestled between jungle and beach.
