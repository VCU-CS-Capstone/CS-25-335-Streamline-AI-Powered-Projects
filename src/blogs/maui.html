Exploring Maui

Surf legendary beaches, chase waterfalls on the Road to Hāna, and watch sunrise above the clouds on the Valley Isle

Must‑Visit Destinations in Maui

Maui blends dramatic volcanic peaks, lush rainforests, and world‑class beaches. Add these spots to your Valley Isle itinerary:

  • Haleakalā National Park: Reserve a permit to witness sunrise from 10,023 ft, then hike into the Mars‑like crater or explore the Kipahulu coastal district.
  • Road to Hāna: Navigate 600+ curves and 50+ one‑lane bridges to discover waterfalls, black‑sand Waiʻānapanapa Beach, and bamboo forests.
  • Kāʻanapali & Lāhainā: Snorkel at Puʻu Kekaʻa (Black Rock) and stroll historic Front Street—mindful of 2023 wildfire recovery efforts.
  • ʻĪao Valley State Monument: View the verdant ʻĪao Needle and learn about the 1790 Battle of Kepaniwai in this sacred valley.
  • Molokini Crater: Take a morning boat to this crescent‑shaped marine reserve for 150‑ft visibility snorkeling with reef sharks and rays.

Maui Cuisine & Dining Experiences

Farm‑fresh and ocean‑to‑table define Maui’s food scene. Sample these island favorites:

  • Ululani’s Shave Ice: Ultra‑fine ice drizzled with house‑made syrups—opt for li hing mui and coconut cream cap.
  • Mama’s Fish House: Iconic Paia restaurant posting the name of the fisherman and catch date on every menu item.
  • O’o Farm Brunch Tour: Harvest your own greens in Upcountry Kula before a wood‑fired feast overlooking Central Maui.
  • Malasadas at T. Komoda Store: Since 1916, this Makawao bakery has served fluffy Portuguese doughnuts and stick doughnuts.
  • Kihei Food Trucks: Hit South Maui Gardens for garlic shrimp plates, poke bowls, and vegan tacos under twinkling lights.

Travel Tips for Maui

Make your Maui adventure seamless with these insights:

  • Best Time to Visit: April–May and Sept–Nov balance pleasant weather and lighter crowds; humpback whales visit Dec–Apr.
  • Car Rental: Essential for exploring beyond resort areas—book early, especially holidays.
  • Reservations: Sunrise at Haleakalā, Waiʻānapanapa State Park, and some popular restaurants require advance bookings.
  • Reef‑Safe Sunscreen: Hawaiʻi law bans certain chemicals—choose mineral SPF and avoid stepping on coral.
  • Respect ʻĀina & Culture: Heed no‑trespassing signs, pack out trash, and learn a few Hawaiian words like mahalo (thank you).

Accommodation Options

Maui lodging ranges from luxe resorts to rustic cabins:

  • Wailea Resorts: Treat yourself at Four Seasons, Andaz, or Grand Wailea with championship golf and sunset infinity pools.
  • West Maui Hotels: Kāʻanapali Beach Hotel and Hyatt Regency offer family‑friendly vibes near snorkeling and shopping.
  • Boutique Inns: Paia Inn steps from surf town cafés, or Hotel Wailea—Hawaiʻi’s only Relais & Châteaux property—for adults‑only serenity.
  • Hāna Hideaways: Slow down at Hāna‑Maui Resort or oceanfront vacation rentals amid East Maui’s rainforests.
  • Cabins & Camping: Reserve wilderness cabins in Haleakalā crater or camp at Hosmer Grove under star‑studded skies.

Cultural Experiences & Events

Immerse yourself in Maui’s vibrant traditions and annual gatherings:

  • Maui Whale Festival (Feb): Celebrate humpback conservation with parades, oceanfront concerts, and educational talks in Kihei.
  • Maui Film Festival (June): Stargaze on the Celestial Cinema lawn in Wailea while watching indie premieres.
  • Friday Town Parties: Rotating monthly block parties (Wailuku, Makawao, Lahaina*, Kihei) spotlight local artisans and live music (*returning post‑recovery).
  • Feast at Lele or Old Lāhainā Lūʻau: Savor imu‑roasted kālua pig and Polynesian dances at Maui’s most authentic lūʻaus.
  • Maui ʻUkulele Festival (Oct): Free concerts at the Maui Arts & Cultural Center showcase Hawaii’s beloved instrument.
