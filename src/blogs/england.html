Exploring England

Cobblestone streets, Pacific sunsets, and jungle adventures on Mexico’s Banderas Bay

Must‑Visit Destinations

Historic landmarks, vibrant cities, and bucolic countryside await:

  • London – Buckingham Palace, Tower of London, Big Ben, and free world‑class museums like the British Museum.
  • Bath – Roman Baths, honey‑hued Georgian crescents, and Thermae Spa rooftop pools.
  • Oxford & Cambridge – Dreaming spires, punt rides, and centuries‑old colleges.
  • The Cotswolds – Stone cottages, rolling hills, and afternoon cream teas.
  • Lake District – England’s largest national park with literary lakeside walks and Scafell Pike hikes.

English Flavors

  • Pub Classics – Fish & chips, Sunday roast with Yorkshire pudding, and locally brewed ales.
  • Afternoon Tea – Finger sandwiches, scones with clotted cream, and pastries at The Savoy or Bettys Tearooms.
  • Street Food – Borough Market’s global bites, from Ethiopian injera to raclette‑laden potatoes.
  • Coastal Seafood – Cornish pasties, Whitstable oysters, and Brighton’s beachside shacks.
  • Modern British – Michelin‑starred spots like The Ledbury champion seasonal farm‑to‑fork produce.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time: May–June & Sept offer mild weather; July–Aug peak crowds.
  • Transport: BritRail Pass covers trains; Oyster card for the London Tube.
  • Weather: Pack layers and an umbrella—sun and showers swap hourly.
  • Currency: British Pound (£); contactless cards accepted almost everywhere.
  • Free Culture: National Gallery, Tate Modern, and Natural History Museum have free entry.

Where to Stay

  • Historic Castles – Book a turret room via the Landmark Trust.
  • Luxury Hotels – The Savoy, Claridge’s, or country‑house Cliveden.
  • B&Bs – Family‑run guesthouses with full English breakfast.
  • Cottages – Self‑catering stone cottages in the Cotswolds or Cornwall.
  • Boutique City Pads – Chic stays in converted warehouses in Shoreditch or Manchester’s Northern Quarter.

Cultural Highlights

  • West End Shows – Catch a musical or Shakespeare at The Globe.
  • Music Festivals – Glastonbury, Reading & Leeds, or classical Proms.
  • Sport – Premier League football, Wimbledon tennis, or Royal Ascot horse racing.
  • Seasonal Events – Christmas markets, Guy Fawkes Night fireworks, or summer solstice at Stonehenge.
  • Literary Trails – Shakespeare’s Stratford‑upon‑Avon, Austen’s Bath, and Brontë country in Yorkshire.
